## Design Context

### Users
Developers and students preparing for technical interviews use this product during focused study sessions. They are trying to find relevant questions quickly, track progress, and shape a prep plan that feels credible and practical.

### Brand Personality
Pragmatic, credible, focused. The interface should feel calm and confident, with a no-fluff tone that supports disciplined preparation instead of hype.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark, technical SaaS with layered surfaces, restrained indigo accents, and strong hierarchy. It should feel intentional and product-grade, not like browser-default UI or a flashy consumer app. Avoid flat near-black blends, washed-out controls, and novelty gradients.

### Design Principles
- Create depth with distinct dark layers instead of collapsing everything into one black plane.
- Use accent color sparingly for primary actions, selected states, and section cues.
- Favor clear spacing rhythm and compact, deliberate controls over divider-heavy layouts.
- Make settings surfaces feel trustworthy and operational, not decorative.
- Keep interactions readable and consistent across filters, pills, inputs, and modal chrome.
